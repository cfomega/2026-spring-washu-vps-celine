Writing Exercises: What is something you appreciate from the past?

I really appreciated MP3 players or old technology. I have looked at my screen time lately and...yikes. I feel that the technologies of the 2000's was great at balance groundedness while using new features of what technology can do for you in terms of productivity and having access to a wider world. With MP3s, they do exactly that. They offer access to a any type of music you want but they are not powered by Wi-Fi or data service. I believe it succeeds over wasting so much time through screens because they elevate the ability of what I want to do, which is listen to music, but not make it my entire experience. I can put any old iPod on shuffle and leave it in the background of what I am doing. Forcing myself to also use earbuds rather than noise cancelling headphones also does not make me completely unaware of my surroundings.

Despite being a toddler when these things existed, I miss how technology did not completely consume people's lives. I can attest that I have spent way too many hours being on my phone rather than doing what I am supposed to be doing. It is personal enough where I can understand its use and use its abilities to the fullest but not be so personal that I cannot unattach myself from it. Music is also meant to be in the background. I scroll even with music playing in the back but MP3s mitigate that. Old tech like this teaches me that you can have a balance of enjoying what technology does without consuming my real life experience and becoming too dependent on it. It instead elevates that by providing a wideset of capabilities with music than without but not needing my full attention 24/7.
