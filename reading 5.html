Reading Response: "My Website is a Shifting House..."

I found the idea of breaking out of the confines of defining a website and into different spaces to be fascinating. It is easy to limit one's creativity by trying to fit into the limits of advertising or blogging. Websites back then were more experimental and personable to the users' needs but have shifted into a more commercialized and rigid form of supplementary media. Instead of falling into these molds, the author suggests treating websites as the author and architect simultaneously. Treating it as the mark of someone's identity with the immerson of providing a seperate space from the rest of the internet. Websites can become their own worlds, if done right.

The author compares different types of spaces or objects such as rooms, shelves, and plants. Websites can have the ability to evolve and capture the essencce of the creator. The personalization is just like the tangibility of filling a physical space and calling it one's own, enabling deeper engagement and self expresson. My favorite comparison was from website to garden. Things take time to grow as much as they take time to code and the placement, arrangement, and details of where to put each flower, stone, or tree is the same as the details of where to put each image, text, or link within a website. If we begin shifting our mindset and treat websites as a living extension of ourselves, then the rigidity of a modern website that lacks connection to a wider scope of people can be solved. Websites are essentially what we make of them. They are individual acts of creativity that is shared to a larger ambition that can consume it and become influenced by it.
