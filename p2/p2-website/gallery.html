WORKS FROM THE WRITINGS

The postcards featured in the "WRITINGS" section of the website. Included in the gallery to focus more on the work itself rather than the written content.

POSTCARD HISTORY ART

Depictions of the craze for postcards in the early twentieth century, the development of the postal service, the growth of the printing industry, and the appearance of shops specializing in the sale of cards.

THE RUSSO-JAPANESE WAR

Images of military engagements as well as of celebrations on the home front at the conclusion of the conflict, when Japan established itself as a major international power.

ARTISTS CARDS

Postcards created by many of the leading artists of the early twentieth century. Many of the postcards are one out of an artist's collection, which is visible through common features of its series.

ART NOUVEAU

Japanese artists were excited by the fluid, curving lines of this sumptuous European style, which they integrated into their own designs.

ART DECO

The style associated with the cosmopolitan urban culture that blossomed in Japan during the 1920s and 1930s. Known for geometric patterns, linear stylization, and saturated colors and a common style used by graphic desiners.

THE WORLD OF HUMOR

Satirical images as well as postcards relating farcical tales, which were produced in sets.

ADVERTISING

Diverse images produced by large and small companies in response to Japan’s growing consumer society. Postcards became a form of promotional media for businesses during this time period.

NEW YEAR'S GREETINGS

Designs created to communicate traditional greetings at the advent of the year. They include zodiac symbols or activities related to celebrating the new year.
