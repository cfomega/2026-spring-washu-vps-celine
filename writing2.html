Writing Exercises: What is a man-made creation that brings out the best in you?

I enjoy coloring templates for painting, penciling, or digitally tapping on my iPad. I really like how I just have to focus on the colors and stay within the lines (or not if I want to be more creative and advances with the overlap and shading of colors). I do not have to worry about the end result being 100% original, and I can just enjoy the aesthetics. It offers me a sense of groundedness because I focus on the process rather than the things that worry me outside of coloring. It reminds me to take time in what I do in the present moment and just focus on the routine that offers consistency when everything else feels uncertain or complicated. It fulfills my value of having that space to unwind and can be done alone, where I can enjoy my own presence, or with other people who takes time to bond with me in a calm and quiet way. I think templates are a great way to have a creative outlet without the pressure of having to be original. It hones in on the methodical process of coloring by offering a pre-made space to do so. I think I focus a lot on tools that supports in my ability to stay productive and "work hard." However, this tool was made so that my brain can breathe and not think too much about the end result. It has helped me become my best self by relieving the stress of overthinking what should be made and emphasizes more on the process of mindlessly creating, which has become a source of comfort for me when I am at my best or worst.
