Reading Response: "Do Artifacts Have Ethics?"

Malik interestingly acknowledges that morals and ethics are oftentimes not included in the design process of technology. Realizing this, he pivots that people in tech should start making connections between them, but what does it mean to add a moral and emotional dimension to products? Malik goes to explain that he means treating consumers ethically and publicly, but the author suggest not adding moral dimension at all. At this point of the reading, I was a bit confused since the terms "moral dimension" felt a bit vague, but I am interpreting the reading as whether or not technological products are made, marketed, and used in an ethical way. The article helps define this term further with the emphasis on how the machine itself does not determine the how it is used and treated by society. It is more neutral by nature, so the question should be "what will I do with this tool?"

However, the author points our that this is not the only question to ask when determining the moral dimension of a technology, continuing with a list of the other questions. I found the questions to be very compelling. Some asks for the purposes of the technologies, other ask about the types of knowledge/abilities will be disclosed through it, and the consequences of its use. They vary in other types of questons and specifics but they focus on the result of influencing the world with it or what it enables us as individuals to do. They are great guidelines, especially with the rise of AI and constant debates about authenticity in one's work.
