Reading Response: "The Coming Age of Calm Technology (1996)"

I enjoyed the distinction between cyborgs and rooms. The difference lies in technology enhancing people or their space to create a higher sense of engagement. Cyborgs are typically wearables such as smart glasses and mechanical body parts, while rooms represent smart home technology, voice assistance, and car technology. Both can be used for good and bad intentions and will most likely become more of the future tech that are created. The article insinuates that the best designs are those that becomes immersed into the person or their surroundings. They are not as obvious as looking into a screen or typing on a keyboard. Instead, they emulate the tangible experiences that occurs in our daily lives. Circling back to websites and their impact, they have the potential to be a space that can become a room. We cannot directly attach ourselves into a website, but the screen can be forgotten if the website offers a space that is procured to our senses, such as cinema or special effects that can take the innner digitized world and extend it to the outer world.

The author expresses a preference from room-based technologies, stating that the focus should be less about changing ourselves for a desired function or engagement, and more about changing our surroundings that would enable us to do more. Moreover, the author asks what would those technologies be, offering open-ended thinking that kickstarts the design process that other creators of these room-based technologies have most likely started with.
