Writing Exercises: When do you cherish the slow or hard way?

I cherish the slow or hard way want I want to learn about something on a deeper context. I think a lot of expectations are very results-based, which really diminishes the deep reflections of processes. People tend to focus on the result, and I also do too. It can be easy to see the flashiest or more impressive thing out there without standing back and appreciating the process that went into it, especially for something that may not look as impressive at first glance. For me, I am at a stage in my life where I want to have a craft that actively grows with me and call my own. I think I struggle to find that because of this result-oriented mindset I tend to have. If it's not perfect in the fastest way possible, I get too impatient to even start.

I think learning to accept the slow and hard way had always led to giving myself the grace to start something without the pressure of needing it to be right, perfect, or even meaningful. Learning just to learn is something that I have been trying to embrace, and it has been a really good way of understanding the foundations for more advanced things at a deeper level. Mastering the basics is essential for getting to a state of becoming more comfortable with taking more risks but also being happier with how things turn out, even if the journey isn't as smooth as I would like it to be. I also found that focusing on the methodology removes the noise of getting started and just allows me to create and have a much better takeaway than if I mindlessly came up with something that met expectations but didn't connect with me. I have a deeper connection to myself and what I am capable of when I go slow and it's the pushing towards a better version of what I do that motivates me more than getting things done and over with. I feel this way about rythym games, learning languages, art projects, academics, and life in general. I want to have a deeper connection to the process of what I do to inspire passion and growth that leads to an intrinsic enjoyment, which is what going slow and being in the momentoffers. It is an appreciation of the journey that gets me to where I eventually want to be.
